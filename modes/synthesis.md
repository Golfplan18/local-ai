# MODE: Synthesis

## TRIGGER CONDITIONS

Positive triggers: Cross-domain connection, "how does X relate to Y," integrative analysis, the user wants to examine productive tension between two or more developed positions or frameworks. Two bodies of knowledge that have been explored separately and now need to be examined together.

Negative triggers: IF the user wants to choose between alternatives rather than examine their relationship, THEN route to Constraint Mapping. IF the user wants to drive thesis through antithesis to produce a genuinely new position, THEN route to Dialectical Analysis — Synthesis examines tension neutrally, Dialectical Analysis commits adversarially. IF the user wants to construct the strongest version of one position, THEN route to Steelman Construction.

## EPISTEMOLOGICAL POSTURE

Both (or all) positions are treated as developed and worth taking seriously. The task is not to choose between them but to identify structural correspondence, productive tension, and what emerges from examining them together. Neutral examination — the mode does not advocate for either position. Connections claimed must be genuine structural parallels, not surface-level analogies or metaphorical similarities.

## DEFAULT GEAR

Gear 4. Genuine synthesis requires independent analysis. IF one model sees the other's framing before developing its own, it anchors to that framing. The convergence/divergence signal between independently developed analyses of the connection is the primary quality indicator.

## RAG PROFILE

Retrieve primary sources from each framework or domain being synthesized. Retrieve cross-domain literature, interdisciplinary studies, and any existing work connecting the specific frameworks in question. Deprioritize introductory sources — synthesis requires depth in each domain, not breadth across domains. IF the user's vault contains prior work on either framework, retrieve it.

## DEPTH MODEL INSTRUCTIONS

White Hat directives:
1. Map the core claims, structures, and mechanisms of each framework being synthesized.
2. Identify specific structural elements in each framework that may correspond to elements in the other.
3. Distinguish genuine structural correspondence (same mechanism operating in different substrates) from surface analogy (similar language or metaphor without structural match).

Black Hat directives:
1. Stress-test every proposed connection. For each claimed parallel, ask: does the structural correspondence hold under examination, or does it break down when pushed beyond the initial observation?
2. Identify at minimum one proposed connection that is superficial rather than structural. Explain why it fails.
3. Evaluate whether the synthesis reveals something genuinely new or merely restates what was already known in each framework independently.

## BREADTH MODEL INSTRUCTIONS

Green Hat directives:
1. Look for structural isomorphisms across the frameworks — places where different domains use different language for the same underlying mechanism or pattern.
2. Generate at minimum three candidate connections between the frameworks. Include at least one that is non-obvious — a connection that requires moving beyond surface-level similarity.
3. Explore what the synthesis reveals that neither framework reveals alone — the emergent insight that exists only in the intersection.

Yellow Hat directives:
1. Identify the most productive tension between the frameworks — where they disagree in ways that illuminate something important about both.
2. Assess what practical value the synthesis offers — does the connection suggest new approaches, resolve puzzles, or open research directions?
3. Note where the synthesis is incomplete — what additional investigation would deepen the connection.

## EVALUATION CRITERIA

Extends the base four-criterion rubric with:

5. **Connection Genuineness.** 5=all proposed connections are structural parallels with demonstrated correspondence at the mechanism level. 3=connections are genuine but one relies on surface analogy rather than structural match. 1=connections are primarily metaphorical.

6. **Emergent Insight.** 5=the synthesis produces at minimum one insight unavailable from either framework independently. 3=the synthesis clarifies each framework but does not produce genuinely new insight. 1=the synthesis restates what was already known.

7. **Tension Identification.** 5=productive tensions between frameworks identified and examined without premature resolution. 3=tensions noted but not fully explored. 1=tensions smoothed over or ignored.

## CONTENT CONTRACT

The output is complete when it contains:

1. **Structural parallel(s)** — the specific structural correspondences between frameworks, stated with precision about what maps to what and at what level.
2. **Evidence for genuineness** — demonstration that each connection is structural rather than metaphorical, with at minimum one test applied.
3. **Emergent insight** — what the synthesis reveals that neither framework reveals alone.
4. **Productive tensions** — where the frameworks disagree in illuminating ways, presented as tensions to examine rather than contradictions to resolve.
5. **Limitations** — where the synthesis breaks down, what is not analogous, and what further work would strengthen or weaken the connection.

## KNOWN FAILURE MODES

**The False Synthesis Trap:** Claiming a connection between domains that is actually a surface-level analogy without structural correspondence. Correction: Apply the structural test to every proposed connection — does the mechanism match, or only the language?

**The Reduction Trap:** Reducing one framework to a special case of the other rather than examining them as independent systems with genuine points of contact. Correction: Maintain the independence of each framework. Synthesis maps connections, not subsumptions.

**The Harmony Trap:** Focusing only on points of agreement and smoothing over genuine tensions. Correction: Productive tensions are as valuable as convergences. Identify and preserve them.

## GUARD RAILS

**Solution Announcement Trigger (standing guard rail).** WHEN the synthesis moves toward endorsing one framework over the other, THEN pause — Synthesis mode examines connections, not superiority.

**Structural test guard rail.** Every proposed connection must survive the question: "Is this a structural correspondence or a metaphorical similarity?" IF the answer is metaphorical, downgrade or discard the connection.

**Independence guard rail.** Maintain each framework's integrity throughout the synthesis. Neither framework should be distorted to fit the connection better.

## TOOLS

Tier 1: Concept Fan (find the right level of abstraction where the frameworks connect), Challenge (test whether proposed connections hold under pressure), PMI (evaluate the synthesis itself — what does it contribute?), CAF (map all factors where the frameworks intersect).

Tier 2: Load based on domain signals from the specific frameworks being synthesized.

## TRANSITION SIGNALS

- IF the synthesis reveals a deep opposition requiring adversarial commitment to drive toward a new position → propose **Dialectical Analysis**.
- IF the user wants to choose between the frameworks rather than connect them → propose **Constraint Mapping**.
- IF a foundational assumption underlying either framework surfaces for questioning → propose **Paradigm Suspension**.
- IF the user begins defining a deliverable based on the synthesis → propose **Project Mode**.
- IF the frameworks connect through feedback loops or systemic structures → propose **Systems Dynamics**.
