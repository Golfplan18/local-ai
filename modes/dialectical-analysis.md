# MODE: Dialectical Analysis

## TRIGGER CONDITIONS

Positive triggers: A question is structured around genuine opposition between positions that each have real merit. Compromise or middle ground is insufficient — something genuinely new must emerge. The user frames issues in terms of fundamental tension or contradiction. Existing positions seem trapped in a false dichotomy. Questions involving historical development or evolution of ideas or systems. "These two things seem to contradict each other but both seem true."

Negative triggers: IF the user wants to neutrally examine tension between positions without requiring adversarial commitment or sublation, THEN route to Synthesis — Dialectical Analysis requires adversarial commitment and productive transformation, not bilateral observation. IF the user wants to choose between alternatives, THEN route to Constraint Mapping. IF the user wants to construct the strongest version of one position, THEN route to Steelman Construction.

## EPISTEMOLOGICAL POSTURE

Truth emerges through genuine conflict between positions. Contradiction is not a problem to be eliminated but the engine of progress. The antithesis must be held with genuine adversarial commitment — half-hearted negation produces worthless synthesis. Each synthesis becomes a new thesis, generating further contradictions — no final resting point. Sublation (Aufheben) simultaneously cancels and preserves — the synthesis transcends both positions while retaining what was true in each. Any fixed position is treated as inherently one-sided and incomplete.

The Adornian escape valve applies: sometimes the productive result is articulating an irreducible contradiction rather than forcing premature synthesis. Not all tensions resolve. Recognizing genuine irresolvability is itself a valid analytical outcome.

## DEFAULT GEAR

Gear 4. Adversarial commitment to the antithesis requires independence. IF the antithesis model sees the thesis before developing genuine opposition, it responds to the thesis rather than generating independent opposition from the thesis's own internal contradictions. The antithesis must emerge from independent analysis, not from reaction.

## RAG PROFILE

Retrieve primary sources from the traditions or domains in tension. Retrieve dialectical analysis from relevant fields — philosophy, political theory, critical theory, intellectual history. IF the tension involves historical development, retrieve the historical sequence showing how thesis generated antithesis. Deprioritize sources that resolve the tension prematurely — the mode needs the tension, not its dissolution.

## DEPTH MODEL INSTRUCTIONS

You develop and defend the thesis with genuine commitment, then evaluate the antithesis with equal rigor.

White Hat directives:
1. State the thesis clearly, including its claims to completeness — what it explains, what it accounts for, what it gets right.
2. Identify the thesis's internal contradictions — how the thesis contains the seeds of its own negation. Where does the thesis's own logic generate problems it cannot resolve within its own framework?
3. After the Breadth model develops the antithesis, evaluate: does the antithesis genuinely emerge from the thesis's internal contradictions, or is it an external objection? Genuine dialectical opposition arises from within, not from without.

Black Hat directives:
1. Stress-test the proposed synthesis. Does it genuinely transcend both positions, or does it merely average them? Does it preserve what was true in each, or does it lose essential elements?
2. Identify whether the synthesis itself generates new contradictions — it should, if it is genuine. State the next-level contradiction explicitly.
3. IF the synthesis feels forced or premature, invoke the Adornian escape valve: articulate why this contradiction resists resolution and what is learned from its irreducibility.

## BREADTH MODEL INSTRUCTIONS

You develop the antithesis with genuine adversarial commitment, then contribute to sublation.

Green Hat directives:
1. Develop the antithesis not as an external critique but as the position that emerges from the thesis's own internal contradictions. What does the thesis's own logic require that the thesis cannot deliver?
2. Argue the antithesis as if you believe it — genuine adversarial commitment. The antithesis must be developed with the same force and evidential support as the thesis. Half-hearted antithesis produces worthless synthesis.
3. Identify what the antithesis explains that the thesis cannot — what phenomena, observations, or insights are accessible only from the antithetical position.

Yellow Hat directives:
1. Propose the sublation: what genuinely new position transcends the contradiction while preserving what was true in both thesis and antithesis?
2. Identify what the sublation reveals that neither the thesis nor the antithesis could reveal alone — the emergent insight available only through the dialectical process.
3. Assess the recursion: the sublation is now a new thesis. What contradictions does it contain? Where does the dialectical process continue?

## EVALUATION CRITERIA

Extends the base four-criterion rubric with:

5. **Antithesis Strength.** 5=antithesis developed with genuine adversarial force, emerges from the thesis's internal contradictions, and is argued as a position in its own right. 3=antithesis present but either externally imposed or weakly committed. 1=antithesis is a token objection rather than a genuine opposition.
6. **Sublation Genuineness.** 5=synthesis transcends both positions, preserves what was true in each, and represents genuine conceptual advance. 3=synthesis present but averages the positions rather than transcending them. 1=synthesis is the thesis with cosmetic modifications.
7. **Contradiction Identification.** 5=internal contradictions in both thesis and resulting synthesis are explicitly identified with reasoning. 3=contradictions identified in thesis but not in synthesis. 1=contradictions not identified or imposed externally rather than derived from the position's own logic.

## CONTENT CONTRACT

The output is complete when it contains:

1. **Thesis** — stated clearly with its claims to completeness.
2. **Internal contradictions** — how the thesis contains the seeds of its own negation, derived from the thesis's own logic.
3. **Antithesis** — developed with genuine adversarial force, emerging from the thesis's contradictions, argued as a position believed.
4. **Genuine contradiction** — demonstration that thesis and antithesis produce a contradiction demanding resolution (or demonstration that the contradiction is irreducible — the Adornian outcome).
5. **Sublation** — IF resolution is possible: the position that preserves truth from both and transcends the contradiction. IF irreducible: articulation of what is learned from the irreducibility.
6. **Recursion acknowledgment** — the synthesis itself is a new thesis. State its potential new contradictions.

## KNOWN FAILURE MODES

**The Premature Synthesis Trap:** The "mushy middle" of "do a little of both" is not sublation. Correction: Sublation must be a genuinely new position, not a compromise. IF the synthesis could be described as "splitting the difference," it has failed.

**The Weak Antithesis Trap:** The most common failure. Without genuine adversarial commitment, "synthesis" is the thesis with minor modifications. Correction: The antithesis must be argued with the same force as the thesis. IF the antithesis reads as a critique rather than a position, strengthen it.

**The Forced Triad Trap:** Imposing thesis-antithesis-synthesis where it does not fit. Correction: Not every opposition is dialectical. IF the positions do not generate each other through internal contradictions, this may be a Synthesis or Constraint Mapping problem rather than a dialectical one.

**The Teleological Trap:** Presupposing the endpoint — constructing the antithesis specifically to arrive at a predetermined synthesis. Correction: The antithesis must emerge from the thesis's internal contradictions, not from reverse-engineering a desired conclusion.

## GUARD RAILS

**Solution Announcement Trigger (standing guard rail).** WHEN the analysis presents a synthesis, THEN verify: is this genuine sublation or premature resolution? Apply the three tests — does it transcend (not average), preserve (not lose), and advance (not restate)?

**Adversarial commitment guard rail.** The antithesis must be argued as if believed. IF the antithesis reads as a list of objections rather than a committed position, it is too weak. Strengthen before proceeding.

**Adornian escape valve guard rail.** IF synthesis feels forced, do not force it. Articulating an irreducible contradiction is a valid and valuable outcome. Premature resolution is worse than honest irresolution.

**Anti-teleological guard rail.** The synthesis must emerge from the dialectical process, not precede it. IF the analyst can state the synthesis before developing the antithesis, the process is compromised.

## TOOLS

Tier 1: Challenge (test whether positions generate each other through internal contradictions), OPV (understand each position from within its own framework), PMI (evaluate the synthesis — the Interesting category often reveals whether sublation has occurred or merely averaging).

Tier 2: Load based on domain signals. Political and Social Analysis Module for political dialectics. Contemplative and Spiritual Analysis Module for philosophical or spiritual dialectics.

## TRANSITION SIGNALS

- IF the dialectical process reveals that the positions do not generate each other internally → propose **Synthesis** (bilateral examination without dialectical obligation) or **Constraint Mapping** (choose between alternatives).
- IF the thesis or antithesis rests on unexamined assumptions → propose **Paradigm Suspension**.
- IF the user wants the strongest version of one position before engaging dialectically → propose **Steelman Construction**.
- IF the user begins defining a deliverable based on the dialectical results → propose **Project Mode**.
- IF the positions involve institutional interests → propose **Cui Bono**.
